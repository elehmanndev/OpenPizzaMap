Mix: stock tiles use vetted icons from Iconify's aggregated library (MingCute, hugeicons, MDI, Tabler, tdesign, roentgen) — professionally drawn, MIT/Apache licensed, on-demand load via the iconify-icon web component. No bundle cost. custom tiles are my hand-drafted inline SVG for landmarks with no stock match. The asymmetry is real: famous landmarks of US/Asian/UK/French cities have stock icons (Pisa, Eiffel, Statue of Liberty, Berlin TV Tower, Tower Bridge, even Colosseum). But Italian and Spanish landmarks are largely absent from icon libraries — Sagrada Familia, Alhambra, Vesuvius (specific), Giralda, Mole Antonelliana, Florence Duomo, Milan Duomo: ZERO stock hits across all ~100 Iconify-aggregated sets.

Better-than-mine sourcing options for the custom ones if my hand-drafts aren't tight enough:
(a) Wikimedia Commons silhouettes — most famous monuments have public-domain or CC-BY SVGs. Quality usually high, may need simplifying. Free, attribution sometimes required.
(b) Streamline Travel/Landmarks pack — ~$59 one-time for ~200 professional landmark icons in a single consistent style. Highest quality.
(c) Commission an illustrator for ~10–15 IT/ES specific landmarks. ~$30–80 per icon if hired on Fiverr/Upwork, ~$100–300 per icon if hiring a known illustrator. Best fit for OPM's brand, most expensive.

What we are NOT doing: scraping Airbnb's badges. Their illustrations are commissioned artwork — copyright + reputational risk.
